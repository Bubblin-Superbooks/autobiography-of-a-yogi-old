Each raga has a minimum of five notes: a leading note (vadi or king), a secondary note (samavadi or prime minister), helping notes (anuvadi , attendants), and a dissonant note (vivadi , the enemy).

Each one of the six basic ragas has a natural correspondence with a certain hour of the day, season of the year, and a presiding deity who bestows a particular potency. Thus, (1) the HindoleRaga is heard only at dawn in the spring, to evoke the mood of universal love; (2) DeepakaRaga is played during the evening in summer, to arouse compassion; (3) MeghaRaga is a melody for midday in the rainy season, to summon courage; (4) BhairavaRaga is played in the mornings of August, September, October, to achieve tranquillity; (5) SriRaga is reserved for autumn twilights, to attain pure love; (6) MalkounsaRaga is heard at midnights in winter, for valor.
