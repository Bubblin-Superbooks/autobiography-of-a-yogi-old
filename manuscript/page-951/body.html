Jesus twice unequivocally identified Elijah (Elias) as John: "Elias is come already, and they knew him not. . . . Then the disciples understood that he spake unto them of John the Baptist." Again, Christ says: "For all the prophets and the law prophesied until John. And if ye will receive it, this is Elias, which was for to come." When John denied that he was Elias (Elijah), he meant that in the humble garb of John he came no longer in the outward elevation of Elijah the great guru.
